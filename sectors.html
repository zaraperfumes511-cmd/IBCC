Manufacturing & Industries

The manufacturing sector forms a crucial pillar of Indo-Bhutan trade relations. Bhutan's strategic location and favorable trade policies make it an attractive destination for Indian manufacturers looking to expand their operations. Key areas include textiles, food processing, pharmaceuticals, and consumer goods.

Trade Statistics: Manufacturing goods account for approximately 35% of total bilateral trade, with Indian exports to Bhutan valued at INR 4,847 crores in 2024-25.

Key Opportunities

  • Textile and garment manufacturing
  • Food processing and packaging
  • Pharmaceutical production
  • Automotive components
  • Consumer electronics assembly

Infrastructure Development

Infrastructure development is a cornerstone of India's development partnership with Bhutan. Indian companies have been instrumental in building roads, bridges, airports, and power projects that have transformed Bhutan's connectivity and energy landscape.

Major Projects: India has committed over INR 8,500 crores for infrastructure development in Bhutan during the 12th Five Year Plan (2018-2023), covering roads, bridges, and power infrastructure.

Focus Areas

  • Road and highway construction
  • Hydropower projects
  • Airport development
  • Urban infrastructure
  • Telecommunication networks

Agriculture & Allied Services

Agriculture remains the backbone of Bhutan's economy, employing over 50% of the population. IBCC facilitates trade in agricultural products, promotes organic farming practices, and supports the development of agro-processing industries that benefit farmers in both countries.

Trade Volume: Agricultural and allied products trade between India and Bhutan reached INR 892 crores in 2024-25, with significant growth in organic produce exports from Bhutan.

Key Products

  • Organic fruits and vegetables
  • Dairy products
  • Cardamom and spices
  • Rice and grains
  • Agro-processing equipment

Engineering & Construction

Indian engineering and construction companies have played a vital role in Bhutan's development journey. From building iconic structures to providing technical consultancy, this sector represents a significant portion of Indian investment in Bhutan.

Employment: The engineering and construction sector employs over 15,000 Bhutanese workers and contributes significantly to skill development in the country.

Services Offered

  • Civil engineering and construction
  • Mechanical and electrical engineering
  • Project management consultancy
  • Technical training and skill development
  • Equipment supply and maintenance

IT & ITES

The IT sector represents a growing area of cooperation between India and Bhutan. With Bhutan's focus on digital transformation and India's strength in technology services, there is immense potential for collaboration in software development, BPO, and digital infrastructure.

Growth: The IT and ITES sector has grown by 25% year-over-year, with Indian companies establishing development centers in Bhutan to leverage the skilled workforce.

Areas of Collaboration

  • Software development services
  • Business Process Outsourcing (BPO)
  • Digital transformation consulting
  • IT infrastructure development
  • E-governance solutions

Tourism

Tourism is a vital sector for Bhutan's economy, and India remains the largest source of tourist arrivals. IBCC works to promote tourism cooperation, facilitate travel arrangements, and support hospitality businesses in both countries.

Tourist Arrivals: Indian tourists accounted for over 70% of total tourist arrivals in Bhutan in 2024, with approximately 125,000 visitors contributing significantly to the economy.

Collaboration Areas

  • Tour operator partnerships
  • Hotel and hospitality management
  • Eco-tourism initiatives
  • Cultural exchange programs
  • Travel and logistics services
